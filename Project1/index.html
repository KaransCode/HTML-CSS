The Tribute

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam BR
(15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015)

11th President of India
In office
25 July 2002 - 25 July 2007

Missile Man Of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, to a Tamil Muslim family. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, and later earned his master’s degree from the Madras Institute of Technology. Abdul Kalam began his career as an aerospace engineer at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a crucial role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. His notable works include the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, under the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) government. During his tenure, he focused on promoting education, innovation, and rural development. He was known for his simplicity and humility, often traveling by bus and staying in modest accommodations.
